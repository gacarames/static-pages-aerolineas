  • PASSENGERS REQUIRING OXYGEN
    		•
    Aerolíneas Argentinas offers the facility to passengers who require or are prescribed oxygen by their professional adviser on a commercial airline under the following  conditions and procedures:
    Passengers who require oxygen on board are treated as medical cases.
    General Conditions
    The provision of oxygen service is available on flights of Aerolineas Argentinas and Austral líneas aéreas.
    If you need to use oxygen on board, it must be pre booked.
    Availability is limited to one passenger per flight, so availability is limited.
    The transportation of Liquid Oxygen is not permitted on board the aircraft.
    The oxygen can only be supplied by the companies equipment. For safety reasons the use of oxygen tubes, hubs or backpacks that are not owned by Aerolíneas Argentinas or Austral and which are not approved by or the aeronautical authorities are not allowed on our aircraft.
    When passenger is traveling with their own backpack or equipment, these must be empty and be transported in the hold of the aircraft.
    The concentrators may be transported with carryon baggage but are not allowed to be used on board.
    Supplementary oxygen is provided by the company only for use on board during the flight.
    The onboard oxygen supply will be provided only to a continuous flow controlled to 2 or 4 liters per minute. Not able to access oxygen supply requirements discontinuous.
    The airline does not provide oxygen on the ground at airports or at any of the intermediate stages or subsequent flights or further transportation.
    Therefore permanent oxygen-dependent passengers should coordinate with their own local suppliers, with their personal covers, medical care, insurance or health services, the procedures and the provision of supply which will be exclusively under the responsibility of the passenger without any intervention by Aerolineas Argentinas.
    These arrangemts include organizing the ascent and descent of stairs form/to the aircraft from/to an ambulance that has access to the tarmac at any airport including the alternate airport not provided on the original flight plan.
    These arrangemts include organizing the ascent and descent of stairs form/to the aircraft from/to an ambulance that has access to the tarmac at any airport including the alternate airport not provided on the original flight plan.
    At the possibility of delays, landings at alternate airports or delays due to technical reasons; your doctor must take into account the possibility of this occurring and give you the advise for travel by plane with supplementary oxygen assuming responsibility for you to complete the flight safely without risk or setbacks to your health.
    After all the explanation, the Airline will only allow passengers with permamnent oxygen dependency when they have the sufficient autonomy Certified by their physician to tolerate the absence of oxygen from the time of check-in to the boarding of the aircraft and from the disembarkation to the completion of all necessary processes till the departure from the Airport.
    The passenger requiring in-flight supplemental oxygen must travel accompanied by an adult, i.e. for example: doctor, nurse, paramedic, or person older than 18 years, with training and certified in the supply of oxygen.
    No passenger needing any type of medical equipment that requires electricity, neither - in principle - will people with needs of use of medico-electronic equipment on board for your trip will not be allowed.
    Although the medical certificate indicating the aptitude of the companion of the passenger for the flight, it does not automatically entail the acceptance to travel, as the final authorization with medical cases depends solely with the Aerolíneas Argentinas Medical Services.
    Procedures
    Request the INCAD- MEDIF form at the time of making the reservation and the supply of oxygen. The subsequent issuance of a ticket is subject to the authorization for this service.
    Telephone contact in Buenos Aires is 54-11-4909 251154-11-4909 2511/12 Monday to Friday 0900hrs to 2100hrs. To verify reception of faxed copies and make any relevant queries.
    Require to wait for the evaluation of the Medical team regarding fitness to fly.
    The Special Service Unit will advise outcome.
    The Special Handling Unit may require additional information if deemed necessary and will advise if approval for travel has been granted.
    The approval for travel may only be given by the Special Handling Unit in Buenos Aires.

  • WHEEL CHAIRS
    		•
    1- Wheel Chair Transportation Policy
    Aerolíneas Argentinas / Austral have wheel chairs available to make the transportation and movement of handicapped passengers easier, who will be able to travel alone or accompanied by an able person according to his/her disability. Said wheel chairs must be requested at the moment of making the booking, twenty four (24) hours in advance. Additionally, if the circumstances so require it, they will be able to use an elevator for the access to the plane. Likewise, the passengers with reduced mobility will be able to transport their own wheel chair without any additional charge. Please, remember that if you travel with your own wheel chair, Aerolíneas Argentinas / Austral grant you a courtesy service. This service permits to transfer the passenger from the check-in area to the plane and from the plain to the arrival sector. Wheel chairs shall be transported only as luggage in the haul. Aerolíneas Argentinas / Austral only transport manual wheel chairs and fixed or folding chair propelled by non spillable batteries. Aerolíneas Argentinas / Austral DO NOT transport neither wheel chairs propelled by spillable batteries nor wheel chairs propelled by lithium batteries.
    2 - How to find your type of battery
    NON SPILLABLE BATTERIES (Non Spillable)

    The NON SPILLABLE BATTERIES are those that are identified with the following captions. In case that your battery has some of this captions, you will have to consider your battery as SPILLABLE. Captions for batteries NON SPILLABLE
    AGM
    Absorbed Glass Mat
    Non Spillable
    Dry
    Dry Cell
    Gel Cell
    SLA
    Sealed Lead Acid
    Non-Regulated


    SPILLABLE BATTERIES (Wet batteries  spillable)

    Aerolíneas Argentinas / Austral DO NOT TRANSPORT wheel chairs propelled by SPILLABLE BATTERIES as baggage. You will only be able to carry this wheel chair without its battery. SPILLABLE BATTERIES will only be transported as CARGO. For domestic flights, please mail to: jetpaq@aerolineas.com.ar' jetpaq@aerolineas.com.ar or calling at the TELEPHONE +54-11-4130-4000+54-11-4130-4000 and for International flights, please mail to: aerolineascargo@aerolineas.com.ar' aerolineascargo@aerolineas.com or calling at the TELEPHONE +54-11-4480-6396+54-11-4480-6396/ 6264. All those batteries that have no identification of being NOT SPILLABLE (or any of their varieties), shall be considered as SPILLABLE


    LITHIUM BATTERIES

    Aerolíneas Argentinas / Austral DO NOT TRANSPORT wheel chairs propelled by LITHIUM BATTERIES as luggage. You will only be able to transport those types of chairs without its battery. In case you need to transport a LITHIUM BATTERY you will have to consult with the Cargo Area. For domestic flights, please contact by mail to: jetpaq@aerolineas.com.ar', jetpaq@aerolineas.com.ar or calling at the TELEPHONE +54-11-4130-4000+54-11-4130-4000 and for international flights to aerolineascargo@aerolineas.com or please call at the TELEPHONE +54-11-4480-6396+54-11-4480-6396/ 6264.


    3- BOOKING
    Once the booking of your flight has been made, you will have to contact immediately the Company calling at 0810-222-VOLAR, with the purpose of planning your trip to avoid unexpected situations at the Airport. We ask you please inform us at least fortyeight (48) hours in advance before starting your trip on the type of chair to be transported. In case that you make a transfer to another air company, you will have to contact with it and ask for the transportation conditions as regards the wheel chair.
    4- PRESENTATION AT THE AIRPORT
    Please, remember that the day of the trip you will have to be at the airport 120 minutes before the departing time of your domestic flight and 180 minutes before the departing time of your regional or international flight. Fixed or folding wheel chairs propelled by non spillable batteries shall be transported as luggage in the haul, as long as said battery is perfectly located in the wheel chair, disconnected and with its terminals insulated, to avoid short circuits or any other activation of the chair. Manual wheel chairs will also be carried in haul. We advise you to keep all the necessary devices that could be lost in your baggage: cushion, footrest, antidecubitus wheel cushion, among others.

    It is important that you take into account that Aerolíneas Argentinas staff will have to verify vissually your battery in order to confirm that it is working well to be transported on the plane. In case it would be necessary to dismantle any part of the chair, we ask you please come to the airport providing the necessary tools in order to be able to do it.

    5- ARRIVAL
    By general rule, all kind of electric wheel chairs and/or manual chairs must be transported as baggage in the haul. Then, taking into account that you will not be able to ask for the wheel chair nor during a long connecting time neither immediately after leaving the plane. The wheel chair shall be delivered at the arrival room.
